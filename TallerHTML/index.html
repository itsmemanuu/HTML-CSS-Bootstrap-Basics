About me

At school, I enjoyed all tech-related things, but I never really thought about being an engineer. As time passed, I developed projects and skills that would be useful as an engineer but I still couldn’t see that I was born for this. Life was screaming at me, but it wasn’t until I fell in love with the lifestyle that was portrayed in the media that I thought about a life in tech. The Internship (2013) changed my perspective on how a career in computer sciences worked. I remember seeing the main characters trying to figure out how to solve the problems they were assigned in the competition, and I fell in love. I couldn’t get that dream out of my head, I had to be a software engineer. That was the best decision I have ever made. The very first day at Universidad de Los Andes I started to live the dream. Every day I need to solve a new problem and I get to learn new things that challenge my thinking and broaden my perspective. I have worked on group projects that allowed me to connect with others and build meaningful relationships. And I am currently a class monitor of the data structures and algorithms course. Some projects I have worked on are: - Autumn: An app to facilitate thrifting clothes whose main purpose was to lower the impacts of fast fashion. (2021) - Several data analysis applications using data structures such as lists, hash tables, ordered maps and graphs. (2022) - A parser for a made-up programming language for a robot using regular expressions. (2022) - An app to rotate a vector a given amount of bytes using C and assembly. (2022) - An app to manage the database of an imaginary airbnb based business using java and SQL. (2023) - A web page for a Spa using Java, HTML, CSS, TypeScript and Angular. (2023) My interests are binge-watching shows, spending time with my family and friends, and generally just geeky things. I have read the Harry Potter series, watched a lot of Marvel and DC comics content, and played Minesweeper like crazy. I take pride in the fact that I am a feminist and I try every day to be an inspiration to young girls that want to work in tech.
